The NC500

Route 66?

Unofficially styled as "Scotland's answer to Route 66" The North Coast 500 is Scotland's biggest road trip measuring a minimum of 516 miles. The route begins in Inverness and flows along the stunning coastal edges of the North Highlands in one round trip.

It's newer than Route 66 (Established in 2015) but undeniably more scenic.

Bringing together the best of the Highlands from glistening beaches, haunting castles, monumental munros and hundreds more scenic stop-offs, you will be spoilt for choice on this road trip.

Along the way you can detour off the official route and discover coves, beaches and even islands.

Inverness

Inverness to Inverness and traditionally clockwise but some do buck the trend and travel up the East coast first. Many have done it one way and then the other. It's usual to meet people along the way that have travelled the route before.

You can Walk it, ride it or drive it and take as many or as few days as you like. Technically it could be done in a couple of days but where's the fun in that.

Most reckon 5 days is a minimum and even then, most of most days will be spent travelling. The longer you spend, the more you see.

The short route being the minimum 516 miles!

The Drive

There are some stretches of traditional tarmac roads but most of the route is narrow or single track. Some roads are unsuitable for certain types of traffic so, read the signs. I've seen many a large motorhome stuck where it shouldn't be.

Although roads can be narrow, there are plenty of passing places and both locals and fellow travellers are polite and accommodating. There's also good visibility from most of the roads which means that not only can you enjoy the spectacular views but you can also see traffic heading your way.

The red phone boxes are not there for decoration. Mobile phone signals are weak or non-existent. You stand a better chance of a WiFi signal if you stay in a hotel or on an established site.

Getting There

Aside from the route itself, there's the little matter of getting there. Unless You're already in Scotland, you need to factor in the initial journey to Inverness and the journey back again.

Edinburgh to Inverness is a little over 156 miles and London to Edinburgh is over 400 miles.

Edinburgh is a worthwhile stop over for a day or so to break up the journey. There is plenty of accommodation, including campsites and the bus service is excellent. Avoid August - it's Festival and Tattoo month and everything is booked solid.

Choices

Barely 50 miles into the route and there's a dilemma. A fine campsite, pub, hotel and restaurant can be found in Lochinewe, gateway to the Torridon Munros. 6 munros in 3 separate mountain ranges dating back 750 million years.

You can walk the 6 summits in 3 days - if you're fit enough and weather permitting.

We visited in the Summer of 2018 when the rest of the UK was basking in a heatwave. The rainbow gives the game away. Whatever time of year, bring a brolly to Scotland.
