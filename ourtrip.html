Getting There

Getting there and back can add days to your journey. We left the west midlands and broke the journey for 2 nights in Edinburgh and the night before starting the NC500 at the Camping and Caravanning site at Loch Ness.

We spent 2 nights in Edinburgh so we could spend a day in the city itself. It was a few days before the Fringe and Tattoo so we managed to get a campsite booking loch-ness-shores-camping-and-caravanning-club-site near to a very passable Fish and chip shop and on a bus route. The bus service was great and Edinburgh was busily gearing up for the two events. It's smaller than you think so easy to walk around, but can be steep. We took a bus tour, visited the Scottish parliament and bought some souvenirs.

Loch Ness was a brief overnight stop but still a great site with a unique snack bar in an Airstream and fresh local produce for sale in the camp shop.

The Journey Proper

Inverness to Inverness and traditionally clockwise but some do buck the trend and travel up the East coast first. Many have done it one way and then the other. It's usual to meet people along the way that have travelled the route before.

You can Walk it, ride it or drive it and take as many or as few days as you like. Technically it could be done in a couple of days but where's the fun in that.

Most reckon 5 days is a minimum and even then, most of most days will be spent travelling. The longer you spend, the more you see.
