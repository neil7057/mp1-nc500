Route 66?

Unofficially styled as "Scotland's answer to Route 66" The North Coast 500 is Scotland's biggest road trip measuring a minimum of 516 miles. The route begins in Inverness and flows along the stunning coastal edges of the North Highlands in one round trip.

It's newer than Route 66 (Established in 2015) but undeniably more scenic.

Bringing together the best of the Highlands from glistening beaches, haunting castles, monumental munros and hundreds more scenic stop-offs, you will be spoilt for choice on this road trip.

Along the way you can detour off the official route and discover coves, beaches and even islands.

Inverness

Inverness to Inverness and traditionally clockwise but some do buck the trend and travel up the East coast first. Many have done it one way and then the other. It's usual to meet people along the way that have travelled the route before.

You can Walk it, ride it or drive it and take as many or as few days as you like. Technically it could be done in a couple of days but where's the fun in that.

Most reckon 5 days is a minimum and even then, most of most days will be spent travelling. The longer you spend, the more you see.

The short route being the minimum 516 miles!
