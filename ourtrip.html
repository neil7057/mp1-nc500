Our Journey

Day 0

Inverness

Starting point and a quick supermarket shop. Spent a few hours in town and then moved on to first overnight stop.

Small city and more to see but save that for the end of our trip, if there's time and we can find somewhere to park.

Day 1

Dingwall

"Field or meeting place of the thing". Only 15 miles or so but a good first stopping point.

Some scenery along the way and a pretty town to explore at the end of it. The town hall dates from 1745. Overnight Stop

Day 2

Achnasheen

Just a 30 mile or so trip along the A835/A832 to "Field of the storms". Achnasheen is tiny and lies at the crossroads of the road to the highlands or to Skye.

It has a post office and shop, studio gallery and cafe. There's a lodge hotel or you can wild camp. Overnight Stop

Day 3

Kishorn

30 miles or so to Kishorn, a region rather than a specific place but there is a sort of village with a shop.

Loch Kishorn has views towards the Applecross mountains. Overnight Stop

Day 4

Kinlochewe

Another 28 miles and entry point for the Torridon Mountain ranges.

A small village with a Hotel/pub. There's a bike/walking trail around the back of the village. The locals tell me the mountains are "as old as the Earth". Overnight Stop

Day 5

Ullapool

75 mles via the long coastal route to "Ulla's Steading". Great fish and chips and the option of a ferry to Lewis

We stopped for a walk around and a meal but there's lots to reccomend this as an overnight.

Day 6

Knockan

15 miles onwards and to Knockan, most famous for its Crag, part of the National Nature Reserve.

Knockan crag is a globally important geological site where you can "bridge 500 million years of history with your bare hands". Overnight Stop

Day 7

Durness

53 miles and the most north westerly village in the UK mainland. This is truly the North coast with simply stunning sandy beaches, a rocky coastline and turquoise waters.

Stayed at Sango Sands Oasis and cant recommend it highly enough but - Book first! Overnight Stop

Day 8

Thurso

70 miles and 2-3 hours and we're now in Caithness in "Bull water", with its Norse history and further north than the southernmost part of Norway

The sea looks like the Caribbean but feels like the Arctic.

Nearby Scrabster provides ferry services to the Orkneys. Overnight Stop

Day 9

John O' Groats

20 miles to the UK's northeastern tip (Jan de Groot). Boasting luxury appartments, self-catering lodges and a C&C campsite, accomodation isn't an issue.

An iconic signpost, working harbour, shop and cafe. There'sa small fish and chips shack in the harbour.

Overnight stop at Caravan and Camping site.

Day 10

Wick

16 miles and a brief stopover for sightseeing. The route back is 120 miles and fats roads (A99, A9).

There are lots of potential places to stop, plus the oil fields to view from the road.

There's actually a '30 stop route between John O Groats and Inverness' guide.

Day 11

Inverness

Back to the start and a full 120 miles from John O Groats. We chose to carry on back to Edinburgh and stop off at Roslyn Chapel

Although this was a further 165 miles, the roads were good and it took us a good deal nearer home. Overnight Stop at Slatebarns Caravan Park.

Getting There

Getting there and back can add days to your journey. We left the West Midlands and broke the journey for 2 nights in Edinburgh and the night before starting the NC500, at the Camping and Caravanning site at Loch Ness.

Loch Ness was a brief overnight stop but still a great site with a unique snack bar in an Airstream and fresh local produce for sale in the camp shop.

We spent 2 nights in Edinburgh so we could spend a day in the city itself. It was a few days before the Fringe and Tattoo so we managed to get a campsite booking at Morton Hall Campsite near to a very passable Fish and chip shop and on a bus route.

The bus service was great and Edinburgh was busily gearing up for the two events. It's smaller than you think so easy to walk around, but can be steep. We took a bus tour, visited the Scottish parliament and bought some souvenirs.

Every Journey...

Starts with a single step or 'tyre roll' and Inverness is the official start and end point. Inbhir Nis means 'mouth of the River Ness' and is the capital of the highlands and administrative centre of the Highland Council.

A city since 2001, it's still quite small and there's no real need to go into the town itself except maybe for provisions.

Parking is limited and more so if you have a larger or taller vehicle but there is the Eastgate shopping centre and victirian market.

And of course, Inverness has a castle!

Close by is the site of the battle of Culloden; and you'll find that all along the NC500 route, there are plenty of things to entice you to extend your journey time.

Our Route

We chose the traditional clockwise route as we felt there's more to see on the West and North parts of the route than the long journey back from John O' Groats to Inverness.

Having travelled the route, i'd say that the East coast can be as interesting, with pretty villages to see and a great view of the oil industry. The main road to/from Inverness is much faster too.

We had 14 days and had to factor into that the journey form home to/from Inverness. That left 11 days to travel the route itself. That's a good amount of time to drive for a short part of the day, stop off if you want and arrive at base in time for camp setup and an evening meal.

First stop was Dingwall Camping and Caravanning Site. Its only a 26 mile jaunt but we'd spent half a day in Inverness - most of it trying to find a parking space.
